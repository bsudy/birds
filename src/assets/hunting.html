To survive in the open landscape, the kestrel has evolved a specialized technique known as windhovering.

By flapping its wings and fanning its tail against the wind, it creates a stationary platform in mid-air, scanning the terrain for the slightest movement of voles or beetles.

Ultraviolet Vision
Ultraviolette Sicht / Ultraibolya látás

The kestrel's eyes are not merely sharp; they can see into the ultraviolet spectrum. This allows them to track the urine trails of voles, which glow under UV light, leading the predator straight to its prey.
